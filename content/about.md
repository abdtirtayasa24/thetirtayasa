## About Me

I turn operational problems into practical data, automation, and AI solutions.

My career did not begin in technology. I started in customer-facing operations, progressed into team leadership and business support, and experienced firsthand how inefficient processes, fragmented data, and limited tools affect both employees and customers.

That experience led me into data analysis.

I began using Google Sheets, SQL, dashboards, and Python to understand business performance and support better decisions. Over time, I realized that identifying a problem was not enough. A report could explain inefficiency, but it could not remove it.

So I started building.

Today, my work sits at the intersection of business, data, and applied AI. I develop dashboards, data pipelines, workflow automations, internal tools, APIs, AI agents, and integrations using technologies such as Python, PostgreSQL, FastAPI, Google Cloud, Supabase, and self-managed Linux infrastructure.

I describe myself as an **AI Enabler** because my focus is not simply adding AI to a product. I help turn AI from an interesting experiment into something people can actually use—integrated with existing data, workflows, systems, and business objectives.

My strongest contribution is bridging the gap between business needs and technical execution. I can work with the people experiencing a problem, examine the underlying process and data, build a practical solution, deploy it, and continue improving it based on real usage.

I am not interested in technology that only looks impressive in a demonstration. I value systems that reduce manual work, improve visibility, support better decisions, and remain useful after they reach production.

I am still strengthening my engineering depth, and I do not pretend to have mastered every area of software or AI. What I bring is operational experience, analytical thinking, technical curiosity, and the persistence to move from an unclear problem toward a working system.

My approach is simple:

**Understand the problem. Use the data. Build what is useful. Measure the result.**
